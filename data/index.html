Let’s look at the facts

Measuring how cashless a country is, is not easy. Do you base your calculation on the total amount of money that is transferred? Or on the number of payments made? Can you even get an eye all transactions going on in a country? Also, who conducts the study? How trustful can a study done by a credit card institution be. For the main findings here we took the results of a study on cashless payments conducted by the European Central Bank in 2017. The following map shows the percentage of cashless transactions based on the number of payments.

You can’t talk about cashless payments without looking at the topic at a global perspective though. The following data shows the world’s most cashless countries based on day-to-day sales transactions in a country – including bank account and credit transfers. The data is a bit old though. This study done by Mastercard is based on research from 2008 to 2013. Caution that the data may have changed a lot during the last few years – especially uprising countries like for China or South Korea. So take these numbers with a grain of salt.
